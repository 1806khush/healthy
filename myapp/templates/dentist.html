About Our Cardiology Department

Our Cardiology Department offers comprehensive care for heart and vascular conditions. Equipped with the latest technology and staffed by experienced specialists, we provide accurate diagnosis, effective treatment, and supportive care for patients with heart-related health concerns.

Our team of board-certified cardiologists, interventional specialists, cardiac surgeons, and dedicated nursing staff work together to deliver personalized care plans for each patient, ensuring the best possible outcomes and quality of life.

Diagnostic Services

Advanced cardiac testing including echocardiograms, stress tests, and cardiac catheterization.

Interventional Cardiology

Minimally invasive procedures to treat coronary artery disease, heart valve issues, and more.

Cardiac Rehabilitation

Structured programs to help patients recover from heart procedures and improve heart health.

Preventive Cardiology

Risk assessment and management strategies to prevent heart disease and related complications.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our cardiology services and procedures.

What should I bring to my first cardiology appointment?

For your first appointment, please bring:

  • Photo ID and insurance card
  • List of current medications and dosages
  • Medical records including previous cardiac tests or procedures
  • List of your symptoms and when they occur
  • List of questions or concerns you want to discuss
How should I prepare for a cardiac stress test?

To prepare for a cardiac stress test:

  • Avoid eating or drinking for 3 hours before the test (water is okay)
  • Avoid caffeine and tobacco for 24 hours before the test
  • Wear comfortable clothing and walking shoes
  • Continue taking medications unless instructed otherwise by your doctor
  • Inform your doctor of any medical conditions that might affect your ability to exercise
What is the recovery like after angioplasty or stent placement?

Recovery after angioplasty or stent placement typically involves:

  • 4-6 hours of bed rest following the procedure
  • 24-48 hours of limited activity
  • Most patients can return to work within a week
  • You'll need to take prescribed blood-thinning medications
  • Follow-up appointments to monitor your recovery
  • Cardiac rehabilitation may be recommended
Does insurance cover cardiac treatments?

Most insurance plans cover cardiac diagnostic tests and treatments, but coverage varies by provider and plan. Our financial counselors can help you understand your coverage and estimate any out-of-pocket costs. We recommend contacting your insurance provider before scheduled procedures to verify coverage and any pre-authorization requirements.

What are the warning signs of heart problems?

Common warning signs of heart problems include:

  • Chest pain, pressure, or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Pain or discomfort in arms, neck, jaw, or back
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • Fatigue during normal activities
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat
  • Swelling in legs, ankles, or feet

If you experience chest pain or multiple symptoms, seek emergency medical attention immediately.
